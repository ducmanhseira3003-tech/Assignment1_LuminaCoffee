The Art of Pour-Over Coffee

Published on April 2, 2026 • 5 min read

Pour-over coffee is more than just a morning routine; it is a ritual of patience and precision. Unlike automated drip machines, the manual pour-over method allows you to control every variable of the brewing process.

What You Need

  • Freshly roasted coffee beans (light to medium roast recommended)
  • A burr grinder
  • A gooseneck kettle (crucial for water flow control)
  • Filter paper and a V60 or Chemex dripper
  • A digital scale

The Blooming Phase

The most magical part of the process is the "bloom". When you pour the first splash of hot water over fresh grounds, they puff up and release trapped carbon dioxide. This step takes about 30 seconds and is essential for a clean, non-sour cup.

Take your time, enjoy the aroma, and remember that making coffee is an art form. Come visit Lumina Coffee if you want to taste our signature pour-over crafted by professionals!
